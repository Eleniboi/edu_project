## Objective: Understand how roles affect the model’s perspective.

### 1. Write a prompt asking the same question (e.g., "What is climate change?").

**prompt**

"What is programming?"

### 2. Add role instructions for at least 3 personas, such as:

    Scientist → technical explanation

    Journalist → informative for the public

    Critic → skeptical tone.

**my own Personas**
    Scientist → technical explanation

    Teacher → Beginner-friendly

    Critic → skeptical tone

### prompt 1 (Scientist)

"Explain programming as a computer scientist using technical terms"

### prompt 2 (Teacher)

"Explain programming  as a teacher teaching beginners in simple terms"


### prompt 3 (Critic)
"Explain programming  as a critic with a skeptical and analytical tone."

### prompt 1 Response (Scientist)
programming is a formal representation of an algorithm— a finite, deterministic sequence of instructions designed to solve a specific computational problem


### prompt 2 Response (Teacher)
Think of programming as the art of writing a very detailed "To-Do" list for a friend who is incredibly fast but not very bright. That friend is your computer.

### prompt 3 Response (Critic)
programming is often romanticized as a form of digital sorcery—a world of "hackers" in dark rooms bending reality to their will. In reality, programming is a grueling exercise in linguistic pedantry and the management of unintended consequences.

### Comparison Of Output:

- The Scientist response used technical vocabulary such as "formal representation" and "algorithm," and provided a precise, detailed definition, showing a technical and expert perspective.
- The Teacher response used simple language and beginner-friendly examples such as "TO-DO", relating real world analogy for clearer understanding.
- The Critic response had a questioning tone and focused on limitations and challenges, emphasizing its challenges and complexity.
- this shows that personas control the tone, depth, vocabulary , and perspective even when the question remains the same.
