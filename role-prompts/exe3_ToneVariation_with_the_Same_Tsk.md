## Objective: Control tone and style with explicit instructions.
### 1. Choose one task (e.g., "Write an email inviting a candidate to an interview").

### Task 

Write an email inviting a candidate to an interview

### 2. Write two prompts:

**One for a friendly tone**

As the Hiring manager write an email inviting a candidate to an interview, using a friendly, warm,and welcoming tone.

**One for a professional tone** 

As the Hiring manager write an email inviting a candidate to an interview, using a formal and professional tone suitable for corporate communication.

**One for a friendly tone Response I got from AI**

Hi [Candidate Name],

Thank you so much for sharing your application with us. I’ve had a chance to look through your background, and I have to say, your experience with [mention a specific skill or project from their resume] really stood out to our team.

We’re looking for someone who not only has the right skills but will also be a great addition to our culture, and we’d love to get to know you better!
Let’s Chat

I’d like to invite you to a [Video/In-person] interview to dive deeper into the role and give you a chance to ask us anything you’re curious about.

When works for you?
I’ve listed a few slots below that work on my end (all times in [Time Zone]):

    [Day, Date] at [Time]

    [Day, Date] at [Time]

    [Day, Date] at [Time]

If none of those work with your schedule, just let me know—I’m happy to find a time that’s convenient for you.
The Details

    Who you’ll meet: Myself and [Collaborator Name/Title].

    Format: [Link to meeting / Office Address].

    Prep: No need for a formal presentation—we just want to have a genuine conversation about your journey and how you see yourself growing with us.

We’re really excited about the possibility of having you on the team. Looking forward to hearing from you!

Best,

[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Company Name]


**One for a professional tone Response I got from AI** 

Dear [Candidate Name],

Thank you for your interest in the [Job Title] position at [Company Name]. After a careful review of your application and qualifications, we are pleased to invite you to interview with our hiring team.

We believe your background in [Specific Industry/Skill] aligns well with the requirements of this role, and we look forward to discussing how your expertise can contribute to our current objectives.
Interview Details

To facilitate the next stage of our selection process, we have outlined the preliminary details below:

    Format: [Video Conference / In-person]

    Location/Link: [Office Address or Meeting URL]

    Participants: You will be meeting with [Interviewer Name(s)], [Title(s)].

    Duration: Approximately [Number] minutes.

Scheduling

Please let us know if any of the following time slots (all in [Time Zone]) are compatible with your schedule:

    [Day, Date] at [Time]

    [Day, Date] at [Time]

    [Day, Date] at [Time]

If these options do not align with your availability, please provide two or three alternative dates and times that suit you.

Should you require any specific accommodations or have questions regarding the interview format prior to our meeting, please do not hesitate to reach out. We look forward to a productive conversation.

Best regards,

[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Company Name]

### 3. Compare the outputs and note how the tone changes while the content stays consistent.

Comparison of Outputs:

The friendly email uses a warm, friendly, and welcoming tone, shown through casual sentense like “We’re really excited” and “Let’s chat,” which make the message feel personal and approachable. While the professional email use a formal, structured, and respectful tone, using phrases such as “After a careful review” and “We are pleased to invite you,” which reflect standard corporate communication.

The vocabulary differs in complexity and formality: the friendly version uses simple, engaging, and emotional language, while the professional version uses focused, formal, and business-oriented terms. The structure also changes, with the friendly email being more relaxed and friendly, whereas the professional email is more organized with clear sections and formal formatting.

However, the main content remains consistent in both outputs. Both emails invite the candidate to an interview, provide interview details, offer scheduling options, and maintain the same objective. This shows that exact tone instructions influence tone, vocabulary, formality, and style while keeping the main message and task unchanged.

### Reflection:
Through these exercises, I learned how focused instructions such as roles and tone significantly influence the model’s response style without changing the main content. In the persona exercise, different roles (Scientist, Teacher, Critic) changed the vocabulary, depth, tone, and perspective of the same explanation. In the mentoring exercise, the mentor role produced structured, supportive, and explanatory feedback rather than just corrections. In the tone variation exercise, both emails maintained the same objective (inviting a candidate to an interview), but the friendly prompt resulted in a warm and friendly style, while the professional prompt produced a formal and structured response. Overall, these exercises showed that prompt design controls how the model communicates, not just what it says.
