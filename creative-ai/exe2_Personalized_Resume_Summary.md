## Objective: Apply prompting to professional use cases.

### 1. Write a prompt that generates a resume summary based on role and skills. Example: "Create a 3-sentence professional summary for a software engineer skilled in Python and cloud systems."

**FIRST PROMPT**

"Create a 3-sentence professional summary for a software engineer skilled in Python and cloud systems."

### 2. Adjust the prompt for a different role (e.g., teacher, designer, marketer).

**Ajusted prompt**

"Create a 3-sentence professional summary for a Teacher skilled in maths and physics."

### 3. Compare how the outputs change depending on the role.

#### OUTPUTS 

FIRST OUTPUT

"Innovative Software Engineer with over five years of experience specializing in high-performance Python development and cloud-native architecture. Proven expertise in designing scalable backend systems and optimizing AWS infrastructure to reduce operational overhead. Committed to delivering clean, maintainable code and collaborating within Agile teams to solve complex technical challenges."

SECOUND OUTPUT 

"Dedicated Educator with a strong background in Mathematics and Physics, committed to making complex theoretical concepts accessible through hands-on, inquiry-based learning. Expert in designing rigorous curriculum that aligns with academic standards while fostering critical thinking and analytical problem-solving skills in students. Passionate about leveraging educational technology to create an interactive classroom environment that inspires the next generation of scientists and engineers."

### COMPARISON

In terms of vocabulary , the technical role used "hard" nouns (infrastructure, AWS,Python Backend) while the educational role use "process" nouns (inquiry-based learning, curriculum, critical thinking)
