## Objective: Identify and correct prompts that produce wrong or made-up information.

## 1. Write a prompt that produces errors (example: “Give the capitals of five imaginary countries.”).

**error promt**
"list five imaginary nigerian president and the year they ruled"

## 2.Rewrite the prompt so it only accepts correct data (example: “List five real countries and their capitals.”).
**Real prompt**
"list five real nigerian president and the year they ruled"

### Comparison And Differences.

### error prompt
The AI uses "probability." it predicts what a Nigerian name sounds like and what a year in the future looks like. 
The names and dates look well arrange and sounds real but they don't exist
One of the risk factors here is hallucination, if a user asked this without the word "imaginary," the AI might still provide fake names if it couldn't find real ones. 
### Real Prompt 

The AI uses "Fact-Checking." it looks for specific matches in it internal database or the web.
Names like "buhari, Tinubu, Bola" and dates are all real
Hallucination is low in this aspect the request for "real" data triggers a focus search protocol.
